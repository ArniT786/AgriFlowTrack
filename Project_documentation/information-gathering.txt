# Information Gathering

## Purpose

The objective of information gathering was to understand the challenges faced by stakeholders involved in agricultural production, processing, storage, and inventory management.

---

## Information Sources

### Primary Sources

* Farmer discussions
* Processor consultations
* Warehouse manager feedback
* Agricultural inventory observations

### Secondary Sources

* Research papers
* Agricultural journals
* Inventory management studies
* Smart farming publications
* IoT and AI agricultural research

---

## Information Collection Methods

### Observation

The agricultural workflow was observed from:

1. Crop production
2. Harvest collection
3. Packaging process
4. Storage operations
5. Distribution activities

---

### Interviews

Interviews were conducted with:

* Farmers
* Processors
* Warehouse managers

The objective was to identify operational challenges and information gaps.

---

### Literature Review

More than 30 research publications were reviewed focusing on:

* Smart Farming
* IoT-based Agriculture
* Inventory Management
* FEFO Systems
* Shelf-Life Monitoring
* Agricultural Supply Chain Management

---

## Findings

### Production Stage

Identified Problems:

* Lack of centralized seed information
* Inconsistent fertilizer records
* Manual record keeping
* Poor crop tracking

---

### Processing Stage

Identified Problems:

* Packaging information not centralized
* Difficulty tracking production dates
* Limited batch management

---

### Storage Stage

Identified Problems:

* Lack of shelf-life tracking
* Temperature monitoring issues
* Inventory visibility limitations
* Product expiration risks

---

### Distribution Stage

Identified Problems:

* Poor traceability
* Inventory inaccuracies
* Communication gaps among stakeholders

---

## Data Collected

### Agricultural Data

* Crop type
* Seed quantity
* Production date
* Harvest date

### Inventory Data

* Product quantity
* Storage location
* Warehouse information

### Perishable Product Data

* Expiration date
* Shelf life
* Storage temperature
* Packaging information

---

## Gap Analysis

| Existing Process             | Gap Identified      |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Manual inventory tracking    | High error rate     |
| Separate information sources | Lack of integration |
| No FEFO implementation       | Increased waste     |
| Limited storage monitoring   | Product spoilage    |
| Poor communication           | Delayed decisions   |

---

## Conclusion

Information gathering confirmed the need for a centralized agricultural inventory management system capable of integrating production, processing, storage, and inventory operations into a single platform.
