# Problem Statement

## Background

Agricultural supply chains involve multiple stakeholders including farmers, processors, and preservers. Information required for crop production, harvesting, packaging, storage, and distribution is usually scattered across different sources.

This lack of integration creates inefficiencies and causes significant losses of agricultural products.

## Current Problems

### 1. Fragmented Information Sources

Farmers collect information from different sources regarding:

* Seed quantity
* Fertilizer requirements
* Storage methods
* Crop handling procedures

This often leads to inconsistent decision making.

### 2. Poor Inventory Visibility

There is no centralized system to track:

* Crop quantities
* Harvest status
* Inventory levels
* Storage capacity

### 3. Lack of Shelf-Life Monitoring

Perishable agricultural products require:

* Expiration monitoring
* Storage condition monitoring
* FEFO management

Current systems do not provide these capabilities effectively.

### 4. Storage Management Issues

Warehouse managers often lack visibility into:

* Storage temperature
* Humidity conditions
* Storage duration
* Product quality status

### 5. Lack of Communication

There is no effective mechanism for information sharing between:

* Producers
* Processors
* Preservers

This causes delays and operational inefficiencies.

## Business Impact

### Financial Losses

* Crop spoilage
* Inventory waste
* Increased storage costs

### Operational Issues

* Poor decision making
* Delayed processing
* Inefficient inventory management

### Quality Issues

* Product deterioration
* Reduced shelf life
* Customer dissatisfaction

## Proposed Solution

AgriFlowTrack will provide a centralized web-based inventory management platform where stakeholders can:

* Store agricultural information.
* Monitor inventory.
* Track expiration dates.
* Manage storage conditions.
* Implement FEFO inventory control.
* Share information across the supply chain.

## Expected Benefits

* Reduced product waste.
* Improved inventory accuracy.
* Better storage management.
* Faster decision making.
* Increased supply chain transparency.
* Enhanced agricultural productivity.
